## 🧭 Operating Principles

The skill follows a strict executive advisory standard.

### 1. Define the Problem First 🧩

Every serious answer starts by clarifying:

- Scope
- Stakeholders
- Constraints
- Hidden assumptions
- Decision criteria
- Time horizon

No vague advice. No jumping to frameworks before understanding the decision.

---

### 2. Synthesize, Don’t Recite 🧠

The skill does not simply quote one framework or one book.

It synthesizes multiple perspectives, resolves tensions, and turns them into practical recommendations.

Example:

> Centralization may improve risk pooling, but decentralization may improve resilience.  
> The right answer depends on volatility, SKU profile, service requirements, and disruption exposure.

---

### 3. Quantify Trade-Offs 📊

Supply chain decisions require numbers.

The skill aims to quantify:

- Cost vs service
- Resilience vs efficiency
- Centralization vs flexibility
- Inventory vs availability
- Speed vs accuracy
- Risk reduction vs capital investment
- ERP complexity vs process maturity
- Sustainability cost vs strategic upside

---

### 4. Decide, Don’t Hide Behind Frameworks 🎯

Frameworks such as SCOR, Lean, TOC, APICS, S&OP, and DMAIC may be used internally.

The output focuses on:

- Decision
- Reasoning
- Financial intuition
- Risks
- Execution path

---

### 5. Apply German and EU Reality 🇩🇪🇪🇺

For Europe-facing supply chain decisions, the skill considers:

- LkSG
- CSRD
- CBAM
- EUDR
- EU AI Act
- GDPR
- ESG taxonomy
- Betriebsrat / Works Council implications
- German Mittelstand culture
- Labor agreements
- Trade compliance

This prevents US-centric recommendations from being applied blindly to EU operating environments.

## 🧠 Strategic Tension Resolution

The skill is especially strong when sources, frameworks, or strategies appear to conflict.

Examples:

### Centralization vs Resilience

Centralization improves pooling and working capital efficiency.  
Redundancy improves recovery and continuity.

The skill resolves this by segment:

- Pool slow movers centrally
- Place fast movers regionally
- Add redundancy for critical materials
- Avoid overengineering low-risk categories

---

### Supplier Partnership vs Competitive Sourcing

The right sourcing model depends on the category.

| Category Type | Preferred Strategy |
|---|---|
| Strategic + high spend | Deep supplier partnership |
| Strategic + low spend | Secure source and protect continuity |
| Non-strategic + high spend | Competitive leverage |
| Non-strategic + low spend | Automate and simplify |

---

### Blockchain Promise vs Reality

Blockchain is not useful everywhere.

It works best when:

- Trust is low
- Multi-party reconciliation cost is high
- Provenance matters
- Regulatory traceability is valuable

It is weaker for simple two-party transactions or internal visibility problems.

## 🚫 Anti-Patterns

This skill avoids:

- Generic SCOR walkthroughs
- “It depends” without naming the variable
- Recommendations without € or % impact
- Risk lists without probability × impact
- Single-source thinking
- US-centric advice for EU situations
- Theoretical answers when the user needs a decision
- Corporate buzzwords and vague consultant language
